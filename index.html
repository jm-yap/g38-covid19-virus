Problem

People are spreading mis/disinformation that the COVID-19 virus is not real.

Research Question

What false claim contributes most to Filipinos' belief that the COVID-19 virus is not real?

Null Hypothesis

The false claims about COVID-19 are equally likely to contribute to Filipino's disbelief in the virus.

Alternative Hypothesis

Certain false claims about COVID-19 have a greater likelihood of contributing to Filipino's disbelief in the virus compared to others.

Action Plan

Analyze the frequency of tweets about false claims that contribute to Filipinos' belief that the COVID-19 virus is not real.

Statistical Test

Chi Square Test

As we are dealing with the frequency of categorical data, the group implemented the Chi Square test in checking the distribution of each category. From the null hypothesis, we expect that tweets that do not beleive in the COVID-19 virus use various false claims in equal proportion. Therefore, the expected value for each is 150/6 = 25.

Chi Squared Values

The observed and expected frequencies were encoded in the given table below. Using the observed frequencies of each category, we computed the mean and standard deviation using Google Sheet's built-in tools. From there, we manually calculated the Chi Squared values of each category to obtain the sum.

Test for Significance

To test for significance in the data distribution, we performed a goodness-of-fit test in the critical region at a 0.05 significance level and 5 degrees of freedom. With the help of Google Sheet's built-in Right Tail Chi Square Distribution function, we found that the p-value for our dataset is 7.46E-05, which is considerably less than the significance level. Hence, we reject the null hypothesis and accept the alternative hypothesis that states that the categorical data are not in equal proportion.

Standardizing Residuals

The standarized residuals per category were computed through the difference of the observed and expected value, divided by the square root of the expected value. This operation was performed on the sheet. Upon checking the standardized scores, it was found that the false claim of COVID-19 being a financial scheme of private sectors is a major contributor to the disbelief in the virus according to the tweets as it obtained a score greater than +2. On the other hand, the false claim on it being a fear tactic was determined as a weak contributor with a standardized score of less than -2.
